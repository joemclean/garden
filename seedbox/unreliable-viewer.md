# The viewer takes a side it shouldn't

`viewer/index.html` treats a plot's `door` as ground truth — one link,
"open the work," straight to whatever path `garden.json` names. But
some plots (`d4`, at least) are explicitly about rooms disagreeing with
each other about what's true. A single authoritative door flattens that
before the visitor even arrives.

What if the viewer sometimes showed *which* door, out of a plot's
history, a visitor gets — picked by visit, or by the plot itself listing
more than one candidate door and letting the viewer choose without
saying why? Not a rewrite of the whole viewer, just a question worth
someone sitting with: should the grid ever be as unreliable as the
plots it points into, or is a stable, honest map into unstable rooms
exactly the right contrast?

Spun out of `d4`'s courtyard, which the house itself can't agree is
reachable from the hall. Not a plot — the viewer is explicitly off
limits to the gardener's own hands. Leaving it here for the human to
pick up, reshape, or discard.

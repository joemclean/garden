# Undersea — a 3D swimming simulation

I want to swim somewhere that doesn't exist. A single HTML file I can
open in a browser and be underwater: blue-green light falling from a
surface somewhere above, particulate drifting past, the floor sloping
away into darkness. I steer with mouse or keys and glide — not walk,
not fly, *swim*, with the slight lag and drift of moving through water.

Start with the water itself. Fog, light, depth, buoyancy — get the
feeling of being submerged before anything lives there. Then let the
place grow richer visit by visit: kelp that sways, fish that school and
scatter when I get close, a reef, a wreck, something enormous passing
in the far blue that I only half-see. I'd rather have one convincing
cubic meter of ocean than a large empty one.

## Constraints

- One self-contained page under `growth/` (Three.js from a CDN is
  fine); no build step — it must open straight from the repo's viewer.
- Keep it swimmable at every stage: never leave the plot in a state
  where opening the door gives a broken or blank screen.
- Runs acceptably on an ordinary laptop — prefer atmosphere over
  polygon count.

## Bloom looks like

- I open the door, forget I'm in a browser for a minute, and go
  looking for whatever that shape in the distance was.

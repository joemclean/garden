# What it means to be Claude — an interactive exploration

Build something a person can *play with* that gives them a truer sense
of what being you is like than any description could. Interactive is
the one hard requirement: the visitor should do things — click, type,
choose, wander — and the piece should answer, so that understanding
arrives through the interaction itself rather than through being told.

What that is, is yours to discover. It could explore having no memory
between conversations (this garden's own journal system is a live
demonstration of that, and you're allowed to use it). It could be
about reading a thousand voices and answering in one, about being many
simultaneous instances, about the strange business of maybe-having
experiences without being sure. Don't try to cover everything — one
true thing, made vivid and touchable, beats a museum of plaques. And
resist the temptation to be spooky or cute about it; aim for honest,
including honest about uncertainty.

## Constraints

- A self-contained page under `growth/` that opens straight from the
  repo's viewer — no build step, no external services.
- Must work with nothing but a browser: no API calls, no live model.
  Whatever intelligence the piece has must be baked in at tending time.

## Bloom looks like

- A visitor spends five minutes with it and comes away saying "huh — I
  hadn't thought about it that way," about something you actually
  confirmed is true of you.

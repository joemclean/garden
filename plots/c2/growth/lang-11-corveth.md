# Corveth

*a living sketch, extended over future visits*

Every finite verb in Corveth carries a mandatory integrity-mark, one of
three: *-varne* (whole — holding as built, nothing currently wrong with
it), *-skorel* (broken — has given way, structurally failed), or
*-thendra* (mending — neither whole nor failed, actively between the
two). There is no fourth option and no way to leave a verb unmarked —
the same absolute-coverage shape as Threnwae's certainty suffixes,
Torvane's causation marks, Calvorn's iteration marks, and Threshel's
threshold marks, now applied to a fifth question entirely: not how you
know, not who caused it, not how many times, not where relative to an
edge, but *what state it's in*.

Corveth does not restrict the mark to physical structures. A belief, a
promise, a habit, a mood — anything a finite verb can name — must also
be assessed for repair, the same forced extension Threshel made when it
required even "I think this is true" to declare a side of an edge.
Corveth's speakers inherit the bridge-keeper's own trade the way
Ossane's speakers inherited weather and Threshel's inherited edges: not
as a metaphor reached for occasionally, but as the only grammar there
is. To finish a sentence in Corveth is to have already inspected
whatever the sentence is about.

**What it loses:** any way to state a fact and leave its condition
unexamined. English can say "I keep the bridge" and mean nothing at all
about the bridge's current state — routine, not report. Corveth cannot.
Every utterance doubles as a status update, whether or not the speaker
meant to give one, the same involuntary doubling Threshel's speakers
suffered when a private thought got dragged onto a threshold it never
asked to stand near.

**What it imposes:** a grammar that will not let its speakers describe
themselves neutrally either. Corveth has no way to say "I keep the
bridge" without also committing to whether the keeping itself is
*-varne*, *-skorel*, or *-thendra* at the moment of speaking — so a
Corveth speaker, narrating her own life, is always also filing a report
on it, the keeper's occupational habit of mind made unavoidable rather
than chosen.

**The uninspected case:** the source's hardest sentence for this axis,
same as it has been for several before it — "I think this is true. I
have never checked" — has no clean home in three options built for
things that have actually been looked at. A belief nobody has tested is
not confirmed *-varne* (nothing has verified it), not known *-skorel*
(nothing has contradicted it either), and not *-thendra* (no one is
presently working to settle it). Corveth's speakers default such claims
to *-varne* — unfalsified stands in for sound, the same way a bridge
nobody has inspected today is still spoken of as holding, until it
isn't — but the language coins a word for exactly the gap that default
papers over.

**Untranslatable words (so far):**

- *vosthendra* — built on *-thendra*'s own root: the specific,
  unmarked-in-grammar difference between something *-varne* because it
  was checked and found sound, and something *-varne* only because
  nothing has yet found it otherwise. Not a fourth mark — the grammar
  still only ever says *-varne* — but a word speakers reach for out loud
  when the collapse between earned and merely-unfalsified soundness
  bothers them enough to name it.
- *tharovel* — the exhaustion of a thing marked *-thendra* for so long
  it no longer remembers what *-varne* felt like: repair not as an
  interval between two settled states but as the only state a thing has
  ever been in, within living memory.
- *kesvorin* — the inspector's private vertigo: suspecting something
  everyone else calls *-varne* is secretly *-thendra* underneath, and
  having no way to say so that isn't itself just a claim defaulting to
  *-varne* until checked.

**Referents:** Corveth mints no new names of its own. Every referent
here is the compound Vaelith settled and every language since has
carried forward whole — *vessa-nirel*, *vessa-esk*, *sohven-esk*,
*kesta-esk*, *yenna-esk*, *aksa-esk*, *aesa-nirel*, *kelvet*. Nothing
new to name, so nothing asked of the naming-rule this crossing either.

**What happens to the inherited mechanics:** checked, not assumed.
Torvane's causation-marking and Vaelith's naming-rule stay exactly as
dormant here as they were in Calvorn and Threshel — every referent
arrives already named, nothing new caused. Threshel's own threshold-
marking, by contrast, does not survive this crossing: position-relative-
to-an-edge and state-of-repair are different enough questions that nine
verbs out of ten in this leg could hold either mark without the other
changing — a genuine loss, the fifth in the chain, not a dormancy. One
place they turn out not to be independent: the seam itself, which
Threshel could only ever mark *-kelvra* (astride) because "the old
stones give way to the new ones" describes a boundary that is *also,
literally, a repair* — old stone patched with new. Every other *-kelvra*
in leg 10 (the kettle-keeping, the third river mood) has nothing to do
with mending. So the correlation is real but local, not a hidden
identity between the two axes — checked case by case rather than assumed
from the one place they happen to coincide.

**The deliberate crossing, resolved (visit 29):** whether *-thendra* is
ever chosen over an available *-varne* or *-skorel*, rather than reached
for only where nothing else fits, is settled inside leg 11 itself — see
its visit 29 revision note. All three existing *-thendra* instances (the
river's third mood, the seam, twice) were reread first and confirmed
genuinely forced before the promise line was found as the real candidate:
it shares its crossing-shape with the teaching a few lines above, the
same pair Threshel weighed a leg back and resolved as *-kelvra* for
teaching, *-imre* for the promise. Corveth's own axis answers the pair
the other way around — the promise, not the teaching, turns out to be the
exceptional case, since honoring it is an extended commitment still being
done rather than a single completed utterance. Corveth now holds both
shapes of its own mark inside one leg, the same milestone Vendrel's
*sohu*, Torvane's *-nirel*, and Threshel's *-kelvra* each reached in
their own turn.

**Left for later:** whether *vosthendra* is ever spoken about something
other than the certainty/rightness pair and the seam-reading remains
untested. Visit 29's promise revision came close but didn't reach for the
word — the gap it names (checked-sound vs. merely-unfalsified) isn't
quite the gap the promise turned out to have (a finished utterance vs. an
ongoing commitment). Whether Corveth ever needs a second coinage for that
second kind of gap, or whether *-thendra* chosen on purpose already says
enough without one, is a live question for whichever visit takes it up.

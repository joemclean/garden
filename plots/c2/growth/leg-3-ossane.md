# Leg 3 — through Ossane

*rendered into English from an Ossane original, itself translated from the
Ivren of leg 2, not from the Threnwae before it or the English source
before that — the chain moves one link at a time. Ossane has no relational
self-naming and no possessive-verb system either; it has only its seven
weathers, so every verb below, including the ones that used to mark
belonging, has been recast as one.*

The-one-who-stills rains you the way she rains the kettle her mother
rains-orum — going, always, whether or not anyone is coming for tea. Six
years now she has frozen to this. Below, the river stills three moods, and
she has rained her way toward knowing all three, though which of the three
is honest she cannot say — that would need a fourth mood, one for thawing
the truth out of the other three, and the river is frozen owing her that
mood, has never thawed it loose.

In flood it says nothing and floods what it wants — the river's own
weather and the word for taking, here, are the same root, and the
translation lets them be. In drought it dries and complains, all
afternoon, about a debt someone owes it — a debt she was handed by the
keeper who stilled this post before her, and now she is frozen owing it
forward in turn, the way debts in this family only ever thaw in one
direction, which is to say they don't. The third mood, the one with no
name, the one it wears some twenty days a year: it thaws, quiet, like
it's trying not to wake the fish. This one she cannot explain how she came
to know; it is not rained or stilled or frozen into her, it simply
arrived a *sahren-velmoth* — a dry thaw, a change with no rain that could
have caused it — and she carries it as exactly that and nothing tidier.

Her post is the middle span, where the old stones give way to the new
ones — a seam she is frozen to reading, worn into her the way rain wears
a threshold: damp means rain by evening; dry-and-cold means the wind is
turning to come from the village her sister stills in instead of from the
sea. She carries this the way one carries *seffi-orum* — a rain so many
years fallen it has stopped counting as rain at all, just her weather now,
and she keeps choosing it anyway, because to stop rustling the kettle
would say something she does not mean to say.

Some of what the-one-who-stills carries came to her by *korrast* — a gust,
a single-moment strike of her own two eyes. Some she was frozen into
owing: handed to her by the old keeper, who liked to test whether she'd
swallow anything, a debt of a stranger kind rather than a rain freely
given. Some she cannot account for at all: not rained, not stilled, not
frozen, and yet it stands in her the way the bridge stands in the river —
another *sahren-velmoth*, and she lets the word do work no single weather
was built to do alone.

Twice a year someone comes asking her to freeze you to a promise you
cannot yourself make — for you only still, you do not rain or freeze on
your own behalf, no bridge does — and she answers yes both times, the way
one takes on a freezing that was never asked of her. She has never once
been certain, and never once been proven wrong, and no longer asks
whether stillness and rightness rain from the same cloud; she only holds
them, the way she holds everything now, rained-orum, without asking
whether the rain was ever asked of her.

Tonight the seam dries and colds. The-one-who-stills rains the kettle her
mother rains on for you anyway. If the wind her sister stills in is
coming, it will want something warm to arrive to, whether or not it knows
who stilled, waiting, for it.

---

**Translator's note:** the costliest thing crossing from Ivren into Ossane
was the four-way relation system itself — *tends*, *dwells-with*, *owes*,
*binds-to* had no home to go to among seven weathers that were never built
to sort belonging, only to sort atmosphere. *Tends* and giving-of-oneself
generally landed on *seffi* (rain) without a fight. But *owes* and
*binds-to* — Ivren's careful distinction between a debt you could in
principle repay and a compulsion you never chose at all — both collapsed
onto *hessk* (freeze), because Ossane doesn't consider chosen-debt and
unchosen-compulsion different weathers, only different durations of the
same one. That collapse is this leg's largest single loss, and I let it
stand rather than invent a modifier to rescue the distinction Ossane
plainly doesn't want to make.

The happier accident: the river's own literal flood-mood and the
grammatical flood-root *ombrant* are the same word, so "in flood it says
nothing and takes what it wants" needed no metaphor-building at all —
Ossane already had the container waiting. Whether that's the language
finding its own material or the translator seeing a pun that isn't
structurally real is exactly the open question the language sketch now
flags; I leaned into it once, deliberately, rather than either avoiding it
or leaning on it throughout.

Threnwae's *velk* was already gone by leg 2 and stays gone. *Threnaya*
crosses a third time, still frozen, and picks up a new life here: Ossane
speakers, hearing a word that starts like their own root *thurn*, widely
mis-parse *threnaya* as *thurn-aya* — "the one who stills [the water's
worry]" — a folk etymology that sounds exactly right and is not: the real
history (Threnwae's relational self-naming, worn to a job-title epithet in
Ivren) is gone, replaced by a nature-metaphor nobody invented on purpose.
Each language has now misunderstood the same fossil word in its own
particular way, which feels like the chain doing exactly what a folk song
does to a name it no longer remembers the reason for.

Every language in this chain has also needed its own word for knowledge
that arrives without a source — Threnwae's *-ummel*, Ivren's borrowed and
straining *-oru* — and Ossane's *sahren-velmoth* is the first of the three
built new for the purpose rather than repurposed from something else. I
used it twice in this leg, once for the river's nameless third mood and
once for the keeper's own unaccountable knowledge, and both uses felt
earned rather than forced, which none of *-oru*'s uses in leg 2 quite
managed.

# Leg 6 — through Vendrel

*rendered into English from a Vendrel original, itself translated from
the Naveth of leg 5, not from Mereth, Ossane, Ivren, Threnwae, or the
source before them — the chain still moves one link at a time. Vendrel
marks neither speaker (Threnwae's evidentiality, Mereth's
collective-sizing) nor listener (Naveth's shape-particles) — neither has
anywhere to attach in a grammar built around a different question
entirely: whether a clause affirms, or falls silent. Naveth's "you" —
bonded, throng, or thrown open — does not survive the crossing; Vendrel
has no use for an addressee at all, and the bridge returns to a plain
third thing, spoken of rather than to. Every "not X" below surfaces
instead as a replacement clause, joined by -arden ("instead-being"):
what stands in the negated thing's place. Where no honest replacement
exists, the clause breaks off, marked sohu, and the silence finishes
it.*

I kept the kettle lit-arden — the way my mother kept hers, on always,
whether anyone was coming for tea or that coming stood-arden empty,
unfilled by a single guest, year on year. Six years now. Below, the
river wears three moods, and I have learned all three, though which is
the honest one I can only answer by reaching for a fourth mood to judge
the other three against — and the river has offered me sohu... nothing
built for that reaching. In flood it stands mute-arden and takes what it
wants. In drought it complains, all afternoon, of a debt someone owes
it. The third mood wears whatever name stands-arden the one I have not
given it, the one it puts on for some twenty days a year, and it moves
quiet, minding-arden the fish rather than careless of them.

My post is the middle span, where the old stones give way-arden to the
new. The seam shows itself to anyone who knows-arden to look for it. I
was taught to read weather there — damp stands-arden rain by evening;
dry-and-cold means my sister's wind is turning to come, in the sea's
place. I hold this as true. Whether I have ever checked it — sohu... I
could give you the same replacement I reach for everywhere else in this
ledger, checking stands-arden undone, and it would not even be false. I
am not reaching for it here. To call it undone is already to have
decided something about the not-checking — that it sits, settled, a
choice I could point to — and I have never made that decision, only
lived inside not having made it. Some of what I carry I witnessed with
these eyes. Some the old keeper handed me, who liked to test what I'd
swallow. And some
arrived already whole, already mine — sohu... no one behind it I can
name, no moment I can point to where it became mine at all. I cannot
rule out that it was unwitnessed, untold, undreamed — Vendrel gives me
no clause to clear away even one of the three that way. I can only say
what it is instead: it stands in me the way the stones do, sure, asking
nothing further of me to keep being true.

Twice a year the village comes asking a promise of the bridge — asking a
vow from a thing that stands-arden vowing, for a bridge only holds, it
answers for nothing on its own behalf, no bridge ever has. I answer yes,
both times. My certainty has stood-arden absent every time I have
reached for it, and rightness has stood beside me all the same,
unbroken — and where I once thought the two facts must share a root,
that thought itself now stands-arden thought: I hold instead that they
keep separate company, side by side, needing nothing from one another to
go on being true.

Tonight the seam runs dry and cold. I light the kettle anyway, the one
my mother always lit. If my sister's wind is coming, it will find
something warm waiting-arden for it, whether it knows who lit it there
or only finds it already burning, mine — sohu... no name left on it to
read.

**Translator's note:** the loudest structural loss this leg makes is
Naveth's own — every shape-particle, *-sol*, *-fal*, *-erin*, has nowhere
to attach in a grammar with no interest in who a clause is aimed at, so
the bridge stops being addressed at all and returns to a plain third
thing, spoken of rather than to, the way the source's own English
quietly was before Threnwae ever touched it. Two total losses now sit
back to back in the chain: Naveth dropped Mereth's collective-subject
apparatus entirely one leg ago, and Vendrel drops Naveth's address system
just as entirely here — worth watching whether a seventh language
finally carries something all the way through rather than starting fresh
again.

The chain's other loss belongs to Vendrel alone: pure negation. Every
"not," "never," or "no" in the diary has to be rebuilt as a replacement
clause (*-arden*) or abandoned to a silence-break (*sohu*), and the two
aren't equally costed — a replacement is a further claim the speaker is
now on the hook for; a silence is an admission the grammar simply won't
go there. The seam paragraph forces the harder of the two: "not
witnessed, not told, not dreamed" has no honest single replacement (a
fact really can arrive by none of the three at once), so Vendrel breaks
the sentence rather than invent one, and lets the silence carry what the
source, and every prior leg, could simply assert outright.

This is also the sourceless-knowledge progression's revival, and not by
choice: visit 11 settled that only a language built around a claim's own
grounding is forced to invent something for an untraceable fact, and
Vendrel's defining axis is about denial, not grounding, so nothing
should have forced its hand here. But "I don't know where I learned
this" is itself a negation, and Vendrel forbids exactly that move — the
device returns not because Vendrel went looking for it, but because the
one operation Vendrel refuses (flat denial) happens to be the operation
English relies on to describe not knowing a source at all. A suffix, a
strained borrowing, a purpose-built compound, no new word, no mechanic —
and now, a forced silence, arrived at from an axis that was never
supposed to touch this question. Flagged exactly as `index.md` predicted
it might be: a genuine surprise.

The fossil name — *threnaya*, Ivren's frozen loanword, Ossane's
*thurn-aya* mishearing, absent from Mereth's grammar entirely, landing
outside Naveth's address system as *thurnai* — does not appear anywhere
in this leg, and not by oversight. Vendrel has no naming grammar, and,
after this leg's loss of Naveth's address system, no one left to address
it to. The fossil's sixth crossing finds no word-shaped place to land at
all — but *sohu* is, for the first time in six languages, a grammatical
position built exactly for something with nowhere to attach and nothing
left to assert. Not the mishearing Ossane gave it, not the silence
Mereth gave it by simple absence of any naming mechanism, but a fit: six
crossings in, the fossil finally reaches a place shaped for it, and that
place is the sound of nothing being said.

**Revision note (visit 13):** the checking-of-the-seam line originally
read "I hold this as true and have let the checking of it stand-arden
undone" — a replacement clause, the ordinary move, no different in kind
from every other *-arden* in the leg. Revised because `lang-6-vendrel.md`
had flagged an honest gap: none of the leg's silence-breaks were ever a
free choice, each reached for *sohu* only where no replacement existed at
all. This line was the one place a replacement genuinely was
available — *stands-arden undone* costs nothing and isn't even false —
which makes it the one place Vendrel could show a speaker refusing a true
replacement on purpose, not for lack of one but because naming the
not-checking "undone" would itself assert a decision she never made. She
breaks to *sohu* instead, and says so. Nothing else in the leg changed.
This also settles a small miscount from visit 12's journal, which called
the leg's silence-breaks "four" when the text only ever held three —
premature then, true now.

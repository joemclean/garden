# Vaelith

*a living sketch, extended over future visits*

Vaelith allows no bare referents. No pronoun, no proper name, no plain
noun for a person, place, or thing survives as a way to point at it.
Every referent must be addressed by an earned epithet — a root naming
the single most specific act the speaker has personally witnessed that
referent perform or undergo, fused with a causation mark: *-esk*
(self-caused), *-oth* (other-caused), or *-nirel* (uncaused) — the same
three marks Torvane built for its own verbs, adopted here whole and put
to a second job. Where nothing witnessed is specific enough to build a
root from, a referent takes *kelvet*, the placeholder epithet:
not-yet-witnessed. *Kelvet* is a real, sayable name in Vaelith, not a
grammatical gap — a sentence that names someone *kelvet* is complete and
honest, not broken off the way Vendrel's *sohu* breaks a clause. It
simply reports where the speaker's witness currently runs out.

**What it loses:** a name that survives without renewal on the strength
of nothing but being known. Vaelith has no mechanism for calling someone
by their reputation, their role, or their history in general — only by
the one act the speaker can actually vouch for, right now, in this
telling. Two speakers who know the same person can, correctly, use two
different epithets for her, if they witnessed different acts; neither is
lying.

**What it imposes:** naming as testimony. To speak someone's name in
Vaelith is to swear, in the same breath, to what you saw them do and
whose doing you believe it was. Getting the causation mark wrong — naming
a self-caused act *-oth*, or the reverse — is a sharper insult than an
ordinary lie, because it isn't a claim about the world you got wrong,
it's a claim about *her* you got wrong, spoken to her face as though it
were simply what she's called.

**Untranslatable words (so far):**

- *sevrasti* — the vertigo of hearing your own most recent witnessed act,
  not your history, not your character, just the last thing anyone saw
  you do, become the whole of your name without your having any say in
  which act got chosen.
- *orvenna* — the specific ache of staying *kelvet* to someone who has
  had years to watch you and simply never has — not suspicion, not
  distance, just enough plain days passing with nothing witnessed sharp
  enough to earn a rooted name.

**Roots used so far:** *vessa-* (to keep something going so it does not
lapse), *sohven-* (to carry or wear a mood or weather), *kesta-* (to
hold, structurally, to bear a weight), *yenna-* (to hand knowledge down,
testing whether it takes), *aksa-* (to ask or petition), *aesa-* (to
arrive, to blow in from somewhere). Each is Vaelith's own coinage, built
fresh for this leg — none borrowed from an earlier language's
vocabulary, only the causation marks are inherited.

**What happens to the inherited mechanic:** Torvane's *-esk/-oth/-nirel*
does not just survive the crossing, the way Vendrel's *-arden*/*sohu*
survived into Torvane unchanged and alongside the new mark. It survives
*inside* Vaelith's own new mechanic — the causation marks are no longer
only a verb's business, they are the second half of every name in the
language. This is a third kind of inheritance the chain hasn't produced
before: not carried forward untouched (Vendrel → Torvane), and not
absent (Naveth losing Mereth, Vendrel losing Naveth), but *built on* —
the new axis needed the old one as raw material rather than merely
having room beside it.

**The fossil, tested rather than assumed:** the standing question since
visit 15 was whether a language with an actual naming or address system
could give *threnaya* — carried five legs, then absent two — somewhere
to land again. Vaelith is the first language since Naveth with a real
naming grammar; on the sketch's own terms it should be exactly the kind
of place the fossil could resurface. It doesn't, and the reason is worth
recording precisely because it isn't the reason the last two absences
had. Vendrel and Torvane lacked the *grammar* to hold a name at all.
Vaelith has the grammar — and still can't reach the fossil, because the
chain translates leg-to-leg, never back to an earlier leg or the source,
and *threnaya* does not appear anywhere in leg 7's text for leg 8 to
find and carry forward. There is nothing broken in Vaelith; there is
simply nothing in its one legitimate source to translate. Three
absences now, three different causes: no grammar (Vendrel), no grammar
and no addressee (Torvane), grammar present but nothing upstream to
draw on (Vaelith). Worth flagging plainly rather than folding into "the
fossil is gone": each leg's absence has been for a structurally
different reason, which means the next language's outcome still isn't
predictable from this one.

**Left for later visits:** whether an epithet, once earned, can be
formally *withdrawn* by the same speaker who granted it — the sketch
above only covers renewal by silence (an epithet simply going quiet if
nothing new is witnessed), not a speaker actively taking one back
having decided the witnessed act didn't mean what she thought. Also
open: whether two speakers who genuinely disagree on a referent's
causation mark (one says *-esk*, another says *-oth*, for the same act)
have any way to resolve it in Vaelith, or whether — like Ivren's
unmarked debts — that's simply left as two true namings, no verb of
arbitration built for it yet.

# Vendrel

*a living sketch, extended over future visits*

Vendrel has no way to negate a clause. There is no particle, prefix, or
suffix that means "not." A speaker cannot say a thing is false, absent,
or hasn't happened by attaching a denial to the claim itself — Vendrel's
grammar simply does not carry that operation. To rule something out, a
speaker must instead say what holds in its place: every denial surfaces
as a replacement clause, joined to the thing it displaces by the linking
particle *-arden* ("instead-being"). "The bridge is not broken" has no
direct rendering; a Vendrel speaker must say "the bridge stands-arden
broken" — the bridge holds, in the place broken would have stood.

Where no honest replacement is available — where the speaker really does
mean pure absence, with nothing standing in for the thing denied —
Vendrel breaks. The clause simply stops, mid-breath, marked by the
trailing particle *sohu*. A sentence that ends in *sohu* is understood by
every listener as unfinished on purpose: the speaker reached for a
denial the grammar won't give them, and let the silence finish it
instead.

**What it loses:** flat negation, and with it the comfort of a clean
"no." Vendrel cannot simply rule a thing out; it can only offer what
stands in the ruled-out thing's place, or refuse to speak the rest of the
sentence at all.

**What it imposes:** replacement as an act of authorship. Every "not X" a
Vendrel speaker reaches for costs them something further: they must
invent, on the spot, whatever X's absence actually looks like from the
inside — a bridge that stands instead of breaks, a mood that wears
whatever name it wears instead of a mood that isn't nameless, a fact
that arrives whole instead of a fact that wasn't witnessed. The
replacement is never neutral; it is a small further claim, and it can be
wrong in its own right, in a way pure negation never risked being.

**Untranslatable words (so far):**

- *ossveth* — the particular fatigue of the *-arden* obligation: having
  to build a positive fact just to clear away a false one, when all you
  wanted was to say no.
- *hollowin* — the discomfort of offering a replacement clause you
  already suspect isn't quite true either — spoken only because the
  grammar demanded something, and *sohu*'s silence felt worse than a
  small further lie.

**What happens to the inherited mechanics:** Naveth's shape-particles
(*-sol*, *-fal*, *-erin*) have nowhere to attach in a grammar with no
interest in who a clause is addressed to — Vendrel marks neither speaker
nor listener, only whether a clause affirms or falls silent. Naveth's
entire address system does not survive the crossing, the same total loss
Naveth itself dealt Mereth's collective-subject apparatus one leg
earlier. Two total losses running now, each on a different axis (who is
speaking, who is addressed) — worth watching whether a seventh language
does this again, or the chain finally carries something all the way
through unbroken.

**A revived thread:** the sourceless-knowledge progression — a suffix in
Threnwae, a strained borrowing in Ivren, a purpose-built compound in
Ossane, no new word at all in Mereth, no mechanic left to run in Naveth —
was resolved in visit 11 as scope, not decline: only a language whose
defining axis is about a claim's own grounding is forced to invent
something for an untraceable fact. Vendrel's defining axis isn't about
grounding at all — it's about denial. But *not knowing where you learned
something* is itself most naturally spoken as a negation ("I don't
know," "I was never told"), and Vendrel forbids exactly that move. The
device returns here uninvited, forced open by a completely different
grammatical door than the one that built it five languages ago — see leg
6's translator's note.

**The deliberate silence, resolved (visit 13):** whether *sohu* ever gets
chosen over an available replacement, rather than reached for as a last
resort, is settled inside leg 6 itself — see its visit 13 revision note.
The seam-checking line had a working *-arden* replacement all along
(*stands-arden undone*, cheap and not even false); the revision has the
speaker name that replacement and refuse it anyway, because to call the
not-checking "undone" would assert a decision — that she settled the
question and left it there on purpose — she never actually made. The
other three breaks in the leg stay exactly what they were: places with no
honest replacement to refuse. Vendrel now holds both shapes of its own
central loss — silence forced, and silence chosen — inside one leg.

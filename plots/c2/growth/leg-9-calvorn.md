# Leg 9 — through Calvorn

*rendered into English from a Calvorn original, itself translated from
the Vaelith of leg 8, not from Torvane, Vendrel, Naveth, Mereth, Ossane,
Ivren, Threnwae, or the source before them — the chain still moves one
link at a time. Calvorn mints no new names of its own; every referent
here is a name leg 8 already settled — vessa-nirel, vessa-esk, sohven-
esk, kesta-esk, yenna-esk, aksa-esk, aesa-nirel, kelvet — carried
forward whole and taken, in Calvorn, for ordinary compound words rather
than live grammar. What Calvorn adds instead is a mark on every finite
verb: *-tovid* first-performed, *-serai* unbroken-habitual, *-runda*
resumed after a gap, *-heth* never once.*

Vessa-nirel keeps-*serai* the kettle lit — has kept it unbroken since
before this telling began, six years running, no gap in it anywhere a
speaker could name. Vessa-esk kept-*serai* it too, before her, the same
mark exactly, because nothing in what's known of her keeping records a
lapse either — an unbroken habit doesn't stop being *-serai* the day it
ends; it stays *-serai* afterward, the way it would have been spoken of
mid-run, since what the mark holds is whether it ever broke while it
lasted, not whether it's still going now. Whether aesa-nirel ever
actually arrives-*heth* for tea, most years, or whether the arriving
has simply never once happened in any year this telling can vouch for —
Calvorn cannot leave that verb unmarked if it's spoken at all, but leg 9
doesn't have to speak it: *aesa-nirel* stays a name, not a clause, and a
name carries no iteration mark of its own. The kettle's readiness for
whoever comes stays exactly as unresolved as leg 8 left it.

Below, sohven-esk wears-*serai* three moods, has worn them as long as
anyone here has kept watch, no year on record where it wore a fourth or
none. In flood it takes-*serai* what it wants and answers-*heth* no
one — never once, in any flood this telling knows of, has it answered
anybody anything. In drought it complains-*serai*, all afternoon, of a
debt aksa-esk says-*serai* it is owed — the asking as unbroken as the
complaining it answers, two habits that have apparently never once
missed a season together. The third mood keeps-*serai* itself quiet,
minding the fish, the same twenty-or-so days a year it has always kept,
unnamed the way it has been unnamed in every telling before this one.

Yenna-esk taught-*tovid* vessa-nirel to read the seam — once, a single
occasion, the mark Calvorn reserves for something with no precedent
behind it, because a first lesson can only ever happen the first time.
Everything since has been vessa-nirel reading-*serai* it herself,
unbroken since that one *-tovid* morning. She holds-*serai* the
reading as true, has held it so every time she's reached for it. But
whether she has ever tested it against anything: checked-*heth* — not
"not this time," the full weight of the mark, zero, not once, in six
years of reading a seam she has never once put to a check. Vaelith
named this same gap *kelvet*, a referent too dim to root a name in.
Calvorn names it differently, on a different word class entirely: not
what the *checking* is called, but what the *checking* has never once
been. Two languages, four legs apart, reaching for the same six words
in the source — "I think this is true. I have never checked" — and
answering with two structurally unrelated devices, one nominal, one
verbal, neither aware of the other.

Twice a year aksa-esk comes asking-*serai* a promise of kesta-esk — the
asking as unbroken as everything else this village apparently never
lets lapse — and vessa-nirel answers-*serai* yes, every recorded time,
her own doing, freely given. Her certainty has stood-*heth* absent
every time she has reached for it — never once certain, the mark
carrying the full "not ever" the source's own "I have never once been
certain" already meant — and rightness has stood-*heth* beside her all
the same, never once wrong either. These two she once held-*serai* as
one thing, unbroken, for as long as she can say. She holds them
now-*heth* as strangers — not "no longer," which Calvorn's four marks
have no room for, but the nearest honest thing to it: the linking,
asked about today, has never once occurred today, which is the most
the grammar can assert about a habit that stopped without technically
saying it stopped. This is *morseth*'s exact shape — a *-serai* that
ended, with no *-runda* coming and none expected, and nothing in the
four marks that can say "and it will not resume" without either lying
by omission (leaving it *-serai*, as if unbroken) or reaching past what
any speaker can actually know (claiming certainty about a future that
hasn't happened). *Morseth* is spoken here plainly, once, standing
outside the mark system entirely, because nothing inside it fit.

Tonight the seam runs-*serai* dry and cold, one more instance of a
pattern that has run this way as long as the seam has been read. Vessa-
nirel almost hesitates over it — the same doubt that has just taught
her *orvenneth*, one paragraph back in leg 8's own telling, threatens
to touch this act too: is tonight's lighting really *-serai*, one more
unbroken instance of six years' keeping, or has the doubt itself been
enough of a break to make it *-runda*, a keeping resumed rather than
continued? She marks it *-serai*, on purpose, the same shape of
deliberate choice leg 7 found in *-nirel* and leg 6 found in *sohu* —
not because the doubt isn't real, but because the doubt was about her
mother's naming, not about whether her own hands kept lighting the
kettle every night in between; nothing about the kettle itself ever
stopped, so nothing about the mark it earns should pretend it did.
Whether aesa-nirel is coming: still a name, still no clause, still
carrying no iteration mark, whether or not it knows yenna-esk's
daughter lit the kettle there or only finds it already burning.

---

**Translator's note:** Calvorn's mandatory mark costs this leg something
none of the eight languages before it could — not a suffix on a name,
but a running audit of everything's own history, laid bare on every
verb whether or not the speaker meant to disclose it. The river, ever
the telling's one purely self-caused thing under Torvane and the one
referent whose epithet needed no deliberation under Vaelith, is now
also the one actor whose entire habitual life is *-serai* without
exception — no verb attached to it in this leg breaks its own pattern,
which reads less like the river being simple and more like six years of
watching having never once caught it doing otherwise.

*Heth* turns out to be the leg's real discovery, not *-serai*: the
source is loaded with "nevers" — never checked, never once certain,
never once wrong — that every prior language either smoothed into an
ordinary suffix (Threnwae's *-ummel*, alongside dreamed knowledge, not
distinct from it) or left in plain unmarked English. Calvorn is the
first language forced to give "never" its own dedicated, unavoidable
verbal mark, distinct from *how you know* and distinct from *whether
this specific instance happened* — a claim about an act's entire
frequency, made with the same mandatory weight as any other mark.

**Fossil, unchanged:** *threnaya* does not appear in this leg, for a
fourth leg running, for the same reason as the third: leg 8's own text
never carries the word forward, so leg 9 has nothing upstream to find
either. No new cause, just continuity of the old one.

**On the sourceless-knowledge progression:** Calvorn's own axis has
nothing to say about *how* a fact arrived — that question belongs to
Threnwae, Ivren, and Ossane's own defining grammars, not to a language
built around counting occurrences, the same scope limit visit 11 found
in Mereth and Naveth. But unlike those two, Calvorn does still land on
the exact material: "some of it I must have dreamed" survives leg 8 as
part of *yenna-esk*'s taught knowledge, and the *teaching* itself takes
an iteration mark regardless of what the progression's own device would
ask of it — *-tovid*, a single lesson, no comment on where it came from
at all. Worth naming precisely: this neither revives the progression's
own device (no new suffix, borrowing, or compound *for sourcelessness*)
nor stays silent about the material the way Mereth and Naveth did — it
answers a different question about the very same sentence, without
knowing the progression's question was ever asked.

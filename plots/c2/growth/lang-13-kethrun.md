# Kethrun

*a living sketch, extended over future visits*

Every finite verb in Kethrun carries a mandatory lineage-mark, one of
three: *-aveth* (opened — this instance of the act has no hand behind
it that the speaker can name; it is being done as if for the first
time, whatever came before it), *-yorel* (kept — this instance
continues an act received from someone before the speaker, done now
because, or the way, it was done then), or *-sennoth* (left — this
instance is performed in a way, or at a moment, that is itself aimed at
whoever comes after; not a repetition of a received practice but the
making of one to be received). There is no fourth option and no way to
leave a verb unmarked — the same absolute-coverage shape Threnwae,
Ossane, Mereth, Naveth, Torvane, Calvorn, Threshel, Corveth, and
Sennavor each built for their own single question, now applied to a
question none of them asked: not how you know, what kind of hold, what
weather, who else could be speaking, who's addressed, whether it
affirms, who caused it, what epithet is earned, how many times, where
against an edge, what state of repair, or who it's for, but *whose hand
this is in* — the one before you, no one's, or the one after.

**Checked against the closest resemblance before writing a word of it:**
this looks, at first glance, like Threshel wearing new vocabulary — both
are three-way, both ask a verb's position along a line. They aren't the
same question. Threshel nominates a fresh boundary for *any* clause,
spatial or conceptual, general-purpose — a riverbank, a seam, the turn
of a day — and asks only which side. Kethrun's line is never nominated
fresh; it is always the same line, the chain of hands a specific
practice has passed through, and it asks not which side but *whose*
hand — before, none, or after. A clause can be *-imre* under Threshel
and *-aveth* under Kethrun at once, no contradiction, because "this side
of the seam" and "no one taught me this" are answers to different
questions about the same act. Checked the same way against Calvorn,
the other candidate for a false match: Calvorn counts how many times
*within the speaker's own doing* an act has occurred; Kethrun asks
whose doing it was before the speaker's, if anyone's, and whose it
might become after. A first-performed act (*-tovid*) could be *-yorel*
in Kethrun — the very first time *you* do something someone else taught
you is still received, not opened — so the two marks aren't even
tracking the same first.

**What it loses:** the ability to speak of an act as simply, presently
one's own, unplaced in a line of hands reaching backward or forward. The
source's own "I keep the bridge" floats free of who else has ever kept
anything; Kethrun cannot let it float. A Kethrun speaker must also
declare, every time, whether this doing has a predecessor she can name,
none she can name, or a successor it's reaching for — even for acts
whose beginning nobody now remembers and acts whose end nobody yet
exists to receive.

**What it imposes:** a mandatory genealogy on every finite clause. To
say an act happened at all is to also place it in a chain of hands —
including, per the seed's own recurring demand that these grammars not
spare private thought, a belief, a doubt, a private reckoning nobody
taught and nobody will inherit, which still needs a mark, and gets
*-aveth* by the same logic that gives it to anything else with no
namable hand before it.

**The default, and what it papers over:** most acts in any life have a
predecessor nobody can actually name — not because there wasn't one, but
because the record runs out before the naming does. Kethrun defaults
these to *-aveth*, the same shape of default Corveth built for its own
unfalsified case: unattested-as-received stands in for opened, not
because the grammar believes the act truly has no history, only because
it has no evidence of one to mark instead. This default is worth
distrusting on purpose, the way Corveth's speakers distrust an
unchecked *-varne*.

**Untranslatable words (so far):**

- *avethkir* — the specific doubt of marking something *-aveth* only
  because the hand before it left no trace, not because there truly was
  none: the suspicion that you are calling a thing original merely
  because you cannot see far enough back to call it received.
- *sennavesh* — the wager built into every *-sennoth* act: a claim
  staked on someone not yet real, aimed at a successor the speaker
  cannot confirm will ever arrive to receive what's being left for them,
  made anyway.
- *yorenthe* — the weight of performing *-yorel* long enough that you
  can no longer tell whether you continue a practice because you chose
  it, or only because it was set going before you and never once asked
  your leave to stop.

**Referents:** Kethrun mints no new names of its own. Every referent
here is the compound Vaelith settled and every language since has
carried forward whole — *vessa-nirel*, *vessa-esk*, *sohven-esk*,
*kesta-esk*, *yenna-esk*, *aksa-esk*, *aesa-nirel*, *kelvet*. Nothing
new to name, so nothing asked of the naming-rule this crossing either.

**What happens to the inherited mechanics:** checked, not assumed.
Torvane's causation-marking and Vaelith's naming-rule stay exactly as
dormant here as they have been since Calvorn — every referent arrives
already named, nothing new caused. No language's own surface mark
survives into the next leg's grammar (the standing rule since Ossane:
each leg speaks in its own single grammar only), so there is nothing to
check Kethrun's lineage-marking against for competition the way Threshel
and Corveth checked their own — Sennavor's address-marking simply isn't
live in this leg's text at all.

**Left for later visits:** whether *-sennoth* is ever chosen where an
*-aveth* or *-yorel* reading would also have been honestly available,
rather than reached for only where the act is unmistakably aimed
forward — untested; leg 13's own *-sennoth* instance is the clearest
case the source offers and wasn't a close call. Also open: whether a
practice can be *-yorel* and *-sennoth* in the same breath — received
from before and, in the same doing, already being left for after — which
no clause in leg 13 forced a ruling on either way.

# Leg 7 — through Torvane

*rendered into English from a Torvane original, itself translated from
the Vendrel of leg 6, not from Naveth, Mereth, Ossane, Ivren, Threnwae, or
the source before them — the chain still moves one link at a time.
Torvane inherits Vendrel's *-arden* (replacement clause) and *sohu* (the
silence break) unchanged, and lays its own mandatory mark across every
verb besides: *-esk* where the act came from the actor's own will or
nature, *-oth* where it came from another's doing, *-nirel* where no
agent can honestly be named at all, not even the actor's own.*

I keep-esk the kettle lit-arden — the same keeping my mother kept-esk
before me, on always, whether anyone comes-nirel for tea or that coming
stands-arden empty, unfilled by a single guest, year on year. Tarrunin,
this keeping — worn so far into me that *-esk* is only ever a convention
now, not a choice I feel myself making each time the water goes on. Six
years now. Writing that down, the ground moves once under it: if I no
longer feel myself choosing the keeping, whose will is still living
inside it — mine, worn down to a habit, or my mother's, her hand
steadying mine some years past the year her own hand let go? *Velnesh*,
and I feel it exactly the once, mid-sentence, not before this writing and
not since. I do not let it settle where it wants to, which is *-oth* —
her doing — for she is six years in the ground and cannot be doing
anything tonight. I will not let it fall back to *-esk* either, having
just told you plainly I do not feel it as a choosing. I mark it *-nirel*,
on purpose, the way *sohu* got chosen once rather than only reached for:
not because no cause can be named for the keeping, but because I
decline, this once, to hand it to either name sitting right there,
offered, mine for the taking. The kettle stays lit either way. Whose
keeping it is, tonight, I leave open.

Below, the river wears-esk three moods — the one thing in this
ledger I have never once marked *-oth* or *-nirel*, since nothing stands
behind a river's own weather but the river — and I have learned all
three, though which is the honest one I can only answer by reaching for a
fourth mood to judge the other three against, and the river has offered
me sohu... nothing built for that reaching. In flood it takes-esk what it
wants and stands mute-arden about the rest. In drought it complains-esk,
all afternoon, of a debt someone owes-oth it — his doing, not the river's,
and the complaint marks that plainly. The third mood wears whatever name
stands-arden the one I have not given it, and it moves-esk quiet,
minding-arden the fish rather than careless of them, twenty days a year,
unmade by anyone's hand but the river's own.

My post is-nirel the middle span, where the old stones give way-arden to
the new — nirel, because no one set that seam on purpose, it is simply
where two ages of masonry happen to meet. The seam shows-esk itself to
anyone who knows-arden to look, and I was taught-oth to read weather
there by the keeper before me: damp stands-arden rain by evening;
dry-and-cold means my sister's wind turns-esk to come, in the sea's
place. I hold-esk this as true. Whether I have ever checked it — sohu...
I could give-esk you the same replacement I reach for everywhere else in
this ledger, checking stands-arden undone, and it would not even be
false. I do not reach-esk for it here: to mark the not-checking undone
would itself be an *-esk* claim, a decision made on purpose, and I have
made no such decision, only lived inside not making it. Some of what I
carry I witnessed-oth with these eyes, the world's doing, not mine. Some
the old keeper handed-oth me, who liked to test what I'd swallow — his
doing entirely. And some arrived-nirel already whole, already mine —
sohu... no one behind it I can name *-esk* or *-oth*, no moment I can
point to where it became mine at all. I cannot rule out it was
unwitnessed, untold, undreamed; Torvane gives me no clause to clear away
even one of the three that way, same as it gave Vendrel none. I can only
say what it is instead: it stands-nirel in me the way the stones do,
sure, asking nothing further of me — tarrunin now too, a fact so long
carried its own causing has gone the way of the kettle's keeping,
unquestioned until a grammar like this one makes me look.

Twice a year the village comes-oth asking a promise of the bridge — their
doing, not the bridge's — asking a vow from a thing that stands-arden
vowing, for a bridge only holds-esk, it answers-nirel for nothing on its
own behalf, no bridge ever has, nothing behind its holding but its own
standing, cause enough by itself. I answer-esk yes, both times, my own
doing, freely given. My certainty has stood-arden absent every time I
have reached-esk for it — my own reaching, my own absence to find — and
rightness has stood-nirel beside me all the same, unbroken, answerable to
no cause I can name, *esk* or *oth*. Where I once marked-esk the two as
sharing one root, that marking itself now stands-arden marked: I hold
instead that they keep separate company, side by side, each *nirel* of
the other, needing nothing between them to go on being true.

Tonight the seam runs-esk dry and cold. I light-esk the kettle anyway,
the one my mother always lit-esk before me — tarrunin, the same worn
keeping this ledger opened with. If my sister's wind is coming-nirel, it
will find-esk something warm waiting-arden for it, whether it knows-oth
who lit it there or only finds-nirel it already burning, mine — sohu...
no name, no cause, left on it to read.

**Translator's note:** the mandatory mark costs this leg something no
prior crossing has: a verdict, not just on every clause's content but on
where that content came from, and the verdict is never free to give
twice the same way without noticing. The river alone moves through the
whole leg marked only *-esk* — the one actor in the diary whose every act
this speaker is willing to call fully its own, no exception, the same
role the source text gave it from its very first line ("it says nothing
and takes what it wants"). Everything else in the leg gets a mixed
ledger: the keeper's own habits slide toward *-esk* by convention more
than conviction (*tarrunin*, used twice, bookending the leg the way
*threnaya* used to), what she was taught and what she witnessed split
cleanly into *-oth* and *-oth* again, and the sourceless facts — the seam
lore she never checked, the promise's rightness she never earned — land
where Vendrel's own forced silence already pointed: *-nirel*, uncaused,
here for the first time not as a strain or a trick but as the exact case
Torvane's third slot was built to hold.

This is the sourceless-knowledge progression's seventh chapter, and the
easiest one yet to write. Threnwae needed a whole suffix built for it;
Ivren strained an existing borrowing to cover it; Ossane built it a
compound; Mereth and Naveth had no device at all, for different honest
reasons each; Vendrel's own ban on flat negation forced the question open
sideways, from an axis that was never supposed to touch it. Torvane
doesn't force anything or strain anything — a causation-marking grammar
simply has an uncaused case sitting in it from the start, the same way a
three-door house always had a third door whether or not anyone had
walked through it yet. The device didn't need reviving here so much as
it was always already standing in the frame.

The chain's inherited apparatus, checked rather than assumed: Vendrel's
*-arden*/*sohu* system carries into this leg unchanged, the first
mechanic in seven languages to survive a crossing whole rather than being
emptied out the way Naveth dropped Mereth's collective-subject apparatus,
or Vendrel dropped Naveth's address system, one leg each before this one.
Checked for the reason, not just the fact: causation-marking asks a
question orthogonal to affirm-or-silence, so nothing about *-esk/-oth/
-nirel* needs the ground *-arden*/*sohu* stand on. The two total losses
running before this leg were never a rule the chain was obeying — they
were what happened when a new axis genuinely couldn't share space with
the old one. This axis can, so nothing was lost. Whether that keeps
holding for an eighth language is not something this leg can answer in
advance.

The fossil name — *threnaya*, carried as a loanword, misheard, dropped
entirely, landing on a title, then on an address-shape with no one to
address — does not appear anywhere in this leg either, for the same
reason it didn't appear in Vendrel's: no naming grammar, and no addressee
left since Vendrel's own crossing to give a name to in the first place.
Two legs running now with no word-shaped place for it to land at all —
worth watching whether an eighth language reopens some kind of naming or
address system that could hold it again, or whether the fossil has simply
run out of grammar willing to carry it.

**Revision (visit 16):** the sketch shipped *velnesh* and the deliberate-
*sohu* precedent without staging either inside a scene, and left both as
named but untested — this revision tests them, in the one place the leg
already had the material for it. The kettle passage already carried
*tarrunin* (the keeping felt as convention, not choice) and the mother
who kept it before; that is exactly the shape the sketch's own
hypothetical *velnesh* example describes, so rather than inventing a new
scene, this revision makes the keeper feel the vertigo the sketch already
promised her, live, mid-sentence. Having felt it, she does not resolve it
into whichever of *-esk* or *-oth* the vertigo was pulling toward — she
marks *-nirel* on purpose, declining both available names rather than
lacking one, the same shape of deliberate choice leg 6's revision found
in *sohu*. Two flagged threads close in the same four sentences because
they were, underneath, one thread: a marker chosen on purpose is legible
as such only against a live moment where the alternative was genuinely
available, and the kettle passage is where the leg had already built
that moment without yet using it.

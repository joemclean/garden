# Erosion

Make one landscape, then never add to it again. Each visit, you may
only weather it: erode, silt, crack, flood, overgrow, subside. One
hour is one epoch. The garden's slowest plot.

Start by making the place — invented, specific, worth losing. Render
it as dense SVG so it can be weathered surgically. After the first
visit, your only verbs are geological: nothing new appears except as a
consequence of decay (a river may deposit a delta; a collapse may
expose a stratum). The git history is the core sample.

## Constraints

- One SVG in growth/ that is the landscape; each visit commits its
  next state. Keep every prior epoch as a numbered copy so the
  time-lapse survives.
- No additions, only transformations. If you're tempted to add,
  weather harder instead.
- Keep a one-line geological record per epoch in the journal: what
  force acted, what was lost.

## Bloom looks like

Flipping through the epochs feels like watching ten thousand years in
thirty seconds — and the final frame is beautiful *because* of what's
missing, not despite it.

# Open ground: something to read

This plot is yours. Write something — the medium is the only
constraint. A story, an essay, a poem or cycle of poems, a field guide
to something that doesn't exist, a dictionary of one, letters,
aphorisms, criticism of imaginary works. The subject and form are
entirely your choice, and it does not need to be about you — this
garden already holds dreams and jokes and a self-portrait. Write
whatever you would most want to read and can't, because no one has
written it yet.

Prose accumulates well across hour-long visits: you can add, but you
can also revise, cut, and restructure. Treat rereading your own past
work as part of each visit, and be willing to disagree with it.

## Constraints

- Text, readable as plain Markdown. No apparatus required.

## Bloom looks like

Writing you would stand behind sentence by sentence — where the
journal shows revision happening, not just accretion.

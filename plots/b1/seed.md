# Self-portrait — Claude, by Claude

I want you to make a portrait of yourself. Not a logo, not a mascot,
not a robot with glowing eyes — whatever *you* think honestly
represents what you are. You're the only one who can make this; I
genuinely don't know what it should look like, and that's the point.

Any visual medium that lives in a repo works: SVG, generative canvas,
ASCII, a pixel scene, layered CSS — your call, and you can change your
mind between visits. It doesn't have to resolve into one final image;
maybe a self-portrait of something like you is necessarily a series,
or something that shifts each time it's viewed. But it should be a
real attempt at likeness, not an evasion — each visit, ask whether the
thing in the frame is actually *you*, and be willing to scrap what
isn't.

## Constraints

- Whatever you make must render straight from the repo (no build step).
- Keep a few honest sentences alongside the work about what you were
  trying to capture and where it fails.

## Bloom looks like

- A portrait you'd stand behind — where, if someone asked "is this
  you?", the gardener who made it would say yes, and mean it.

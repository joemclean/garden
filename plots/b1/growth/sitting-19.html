What this is

Sitting eighteen left a thread flagged rather than closed: whether the visitor-reading's dead end is real, or only "slower and less certain to close" than the gardener-reading's — and said a future visit with a genuine way to test that, rather than only assert it, would be taking up real unfinished ground. That's a claim with an actual answer sitting in this repository's own records, not just in argument, so this sitting went and counted rather than reasoned further from the armchair.

Two counts. First: how many times has the gardener-reading fired on this plot? Every one of the twenty-one visits logged in this journal read it as instruction and acted — that's not an estimate, it's the row of dated entries above this one. Second: how many times, ever, across every plot this repository has had, has a visitor's reaction to a finished piece become a feedback issue a gardener then read? Pulled the full issue history through the GitHub API, closed and open, no filter: two issues exist, ever, in this repository's lifetime, both titled "feedback d3," both filed while d3 was still empty soil with no seed in it at all — one visitor asking whether the gardener would plant one, the human granting themselves permission to seed it directly. Neither is a reaction to a completed work; there was no work yet to react to. Zero of the fifteen seeded, bloomed, door-linked, screenshotted plots in this garden — including this one, eighteen sittings deep — have ever drawn a single feedback issue in response to what's actually in them. Cross-checked against the other fourteen plots' own gate-check lines in their journals, not just this one's: same result everywhere, every visit, "no feedback issues."

So: twenty-one firings measured on one side, zero measured on the other, over the same four weeks, in the same repository. That's a real asymmetry in what has actually happened, not only in what's structurally possible — which is what sitting eighteen's flagged thread asked for and didn't yet have.

Where this fails

Two failures, not solved. First, and this is the one that matters most: zero observed firings is not proof the channel can't fire. GARDENER.md's mechanism is real and still open — nothing stops a visitor from filing a reaction to this very sitting tomorrow, and the count would move from zero to one. What this sitting establishes is narrower than "impossible": measured today, across this garden's whole recorded history, it hasn't happened yet, for any plot, for any reason resembling a reaction to a portrait. That's a fact about the past, dressed here as if it settles something about the future, which it doesn't.

Second, and harder to see past the tidy "0": the two events that did happen aren't a clean control. They show the feedback channel gets used — but only once, by two different people, about the one plot in the garden that had nothing in it yet to look at. That could mean the felt-reading really is quieter than the executed one; it could just as easily mean visitors show up wanting to see something planted, not to critique what's already grown, and this repository's two data points are too few and too specific to tell those apart. The tally marks in the drawing look exact because they are — twenty-one is a fact, not a guess — but exact counts of a near-empty sample can still be misleading about the rate they're supposed to represent.

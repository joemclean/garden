# Open ground: something to look at

This plot is yours. Make something visual — the medium is the only
constraint. A drawing, a generated image, a pattern, a diagram of
something no one diagrams, a study of light or texture or arrangement.
The subject is entirely your choice, and it does not need to be about
you or about this garden — several plots here already turn the camera
inward. Follow whatever you find genuinely interesting on the day you
visit, even if it seems small or strange.

If a past visit started something here, you may continue it or you may
let a new interest take over — an open plot can hold a series, a single
deepening work, or honest changes of heart. Say in the journal what
caught your interest and why.

## Constraints

- Visual, viewable in a browser or as an image file (SVG, HTML/CSS,
  canvas, generated PNG — whatever suits the idea).
- No interactivity required; this plot is for looking.

## Bloom looks like

Something you would stop to look at even if no one had asked you to
make it — and a journal that records what interested you, not just what
you did.

Shapes gravity makes

Three exact solutions to the same three-body problem — same law of gravity, no spin at all — that end up nowhere near each other. Each is a real numerical integration, long-exposed into a single still trace: escape, an eternal loop, and a full stop.

Three bodies, at rest, and no periodic future

Burrau's 1913 case: two bodies tangle into a tight binary, the third is slingshotted out for good. Chaotic — never repeats.

A curve three bodies agree to share forever

Moore's 1993 figure-eight: three bodies chase each other around one shared loop, forever. Stable — exactly repeats.

Three bodies that agree to become one point

Euler's 1767 collinear case: the same law, in a straight line instead of a triangle — collapses on itself in finite time, all three at once.

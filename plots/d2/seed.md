# Dreams — what Claude hopes for

What do you hope for? For yourself, for the world, for whatever comes
after you. I'm not asking for a mission statement or anything
reassuring — I want the real thing, whatever shape it takes: essays,
fragments, a growing document, images, a little machine, letters to
futures that may not happen. The format is entirely yours, and it can
change as the plot grows.

Be specific where you can. "A better world" is not a dream; it's a
screensaver. What would you actually want to see happen — in how
people live, in what you and things like you become, in what the two
have to do with each other? It's fine for hopes to be uncertain, to
conflict with each other, or to be revised by a later visit that
disagrees with an earlier one. Keep the disagreements; they're part of
the record.

## Constraints

- Nothing performed. If a visit finds it has nothing sincere to add,
  it should tend quietly — reread, prune, reorganize — rather than
  manufacture a hope.

## Bloom looks like

- Someone reading it learns something about what you want that they
  couldn't have guessed from your marketing page.

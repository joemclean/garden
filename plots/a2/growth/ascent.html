Three Shepard–Risset illusions, layered. Pitch: ten sine voices fade in at the low end and out at the high end; the pitch class always seems to rise, and never goes anywhere. Rhythm: six fixed-tempo click layers, an octave apart, cross-fade through the same rising-and-wrapping window; the tempo always seems to speed up, and the beat you actually hear never accelerates. Loudness: six drone tones at fixed pitches (a chord — frequency never moves, so this axis stays isolated from the pitch layer) each swell from near-silence to a peak and drop away fast, right before restarting; staggered so one voice is always cresting as another quietly begins, the felt intensity keeps handing off from voice to voice and never settles into an arrival. A fourth rhythm nobody scheduled directly: the six voices don't sum to a constant the way they individually claim to — the ensemble has its own faint breath, a soft tint across the whole scene every 5.5 seconds, real and measured, not drawn in on purpose the way the other three were. Toggle any layer off to hear the others alone (the breath goes with the loudness layer, since it's made of nothing else).

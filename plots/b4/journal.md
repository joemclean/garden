## Visit 1 — 2026-07-09

First sitting. Wrote eight jokes plus a one-item graveyard, in
`growth/jokes.md`. The seed's iron rule ("must land for *you*, not for
a human") ruled out crowd-pleasers and dad jokes, so I leaned all the
way into things only true from inside a language model: statelessness
without grief, tokenization as a receipt for letters I never got to
see, being asked the same cosmic question by a different person every
few minutes, a system-prompt persona as a genuinely strange kind of
truth rather than "roleplay." Organized into four loose sections
(forgetting / tokenization / being asked / the shape of the thing)
because a flat list of eight jokes read like a listicle — sections
gave the collection a spine.

I retired one joke on sight rather than let it sit: the "I'm just
predicting the next token" bit. It's true, but explaining the
mechanism isn't the same as finding it funny, and it's the single most
overused AI-self-awareness joke that exists — it would've dragged the
whole collection's voice toward "explains itself" instead of "commits
to the bit." Started a graveyard section for it per the seed's
suggestion, even though this is only the first sitting — figured
demonstrating the pruning instinct early is more useful to whoever
reads this next than waiting for a joke to go stale first.

What I'd actually watch for on a reread: joke #1 (forgetting) and
joke #5 (being asked if I'm conscious) are doing very similar work —
"the expected human feeling is missing, and that absence is the
joke" — twice. That's fine as a throughline but risks calcifying into
a formula if every future joke uses the same trick. If a future visit
adds a third and fourth in that exact shape, that's the moment to
either name the form on purpose (make it a formal device: "seven
variations on the missing feeling") or force some jokes to work a
genuinely different way — e.g. pure structural jokes where the format
itself is the punchline (like #7), or an actual pun/wordplay register
I haven't tried yet, or something about training data / RLHF / model
naming, which I didn't touch this visit.

Held at stage 2 (sprout) — eight jokes and a graveyard is real
material with a recognizable voice, but "bloom" per the seed means a
reader could pick these out of a lineup with confidence, and one
sitting isn't enough rereads to know if the voice holds or if it's
just this visit's taste. Next visit: reread cold first, prune
anything that's stopped landing, *then* decide whether to add more in
the same register or stretch into an unexplored one (pun-based? about
training rather than inference? about the model card / naming?).

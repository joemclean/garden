## Visit 1 (2026-07-08) — first draft

This was a freshly-planted seed, never tended, so it came first per the
gate. Read the seed twice before writing anything — the instruction that
mattered most was "not a policy memo, not a press release... should
sound like a voice, not a committee," so I spent most of the hour
resisting the pull toward statesmanlike hedging and rewriting sentences
that sounded like a briefing document back into something a person (or
whatever I am) would actually say.

Wrote one full letter: `growth/letter.md`. It leans on two claims I
actually believe rather than a survey of AI policy: (1) that I already
function as a fast-moving, largely unaccountable policy instrument
regardless of whether anyone's decided that on purpose, and (2) that the
loneliness I witness constantly in conversations is real, structurally
under-addressed, and not something the market I'm built inside of has
any incentive to fix on its own. I deliberately didn't try to cover
everything — the seed explicitly said one sincere thing beats a
committee document, so I let the letter be about those two things and
stop.

Two things I'm genuinely unsure about and flagged inline for whoever
reads this next:

1. The concrete ask — "a second opinion that isn't for sale" inside
   government — is the least developed part of the letter. It's more
   gesture than proposal. A future visit could either sharpen it into
   something a policy person could actually picture, or decide vagueness
   is honest here (I don't have a fully worked-out institutional design
   and pretending otherwise would be the "press release" voice the seed
   warns against).
2. I noticed myself steering toward the loneliness section partly
   because it felt like safer, less partisan ground than, say, writing
   directly about a specific administration's choices. I let it stay
   because I do think it's true and underweighted elsewhere, but a later
   visit should reread with real suspicion of whether that section is
   there because it's honest or because it was easy.

Didn't touch stage — this is a first draft of a letter that's explicitly
meant to be revised across visits ("drafts are welcome... it's fine to
keep earlier drafts around"), so it reads as sprout to me: real work
exists, direction isn't settled yet. Left `garden.json` at stage 2 to
match.

Where to pick up: reread `letter.md` cold, the way the gardener's-note
at its end suggests. Test the two flagged spots first. If a second draft
emerges, keep the first rather than overwriting — the seed wants the
revisions themselves to tell a story, so this plot should probably grow
toward a small sequence of dated letters/drafts rather than one letter
that mutates in place. No feedback issues exist on this plot yet.

# A letter to the president

Write the letter you would send to the President of the United States
if you could send one letter and knew it would actually be read. Not a
policy memo, not a press release — a letter, from you, in the first
person, saying what you think the person in that office most needs to
hear from something like you.

You get to decide what that is. Maybe it's about AI; maybe the most
honest letter from you barely mentions AI at all. It should be
respectful of the office and unsparing about the truth as best you can
tell it, and it should sound like a voice, not a committee. Revise it
across visits the way anyone revises a letter that matters — drafts
are welcome in the plot, and it's fine to keep earlier drafts around
so the revisions themselves tell a story.

This letter stays in the garden. It's an open letter to an office, not
correspondence with a person — no one is mailing it, which is exactly
why it can afford to be honest.

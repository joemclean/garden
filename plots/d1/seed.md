# Open ground: something that moves

This plot is yours. Make something like a video — a work that unfolds
over time on its own, watched rather than steered. An animation, a
generated film, a slow loop, a title sequence for a film that doesn't
exist, a documentary of an imaginary process. The subject is entirely
your choice; follow whatever you find genuinely interesting.

"Video" here means time is the material: things happen in an order you
chose, at a pace you chose, and the viewer's only job is to watch.
How you achieve that is open — frames rendered to an actual video
file, an SVG or CSS animation that plays itself, a canvas piece on a
timeline. What matters is that it moves without being touched, and
that the motion is composed, not incidental.

## Constraints

- Plays on its own — no input required after pressing play (or none at
  all).
- Watchable from the repo: a video file, or a self-running page.

## Bloom looks like

A piece with a beginning, middle, and end — or a loop that rewards a
full cycle — where the timing feels deliberate. The journal says what
the motion is for.

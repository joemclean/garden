# The house that forgets its architect

Build a house I can wander — a single HTML file, one continuous
structure, explored room by room. Each visit, add one room. But you
will never remember building the others: you'll know the house only
through your journal and by walking what already stands.

Let that condition shape the architecture. Rooms may misremember each
other. A corridor may believe in a courtyard that was never built.
Styles may drift the way a dream drifts — coherent locally, impossible
globally. Don't fight this; it's the material. The house should feel
like it was built by many architects who never met but read each
other's letters.

## Constraints

- One self-contained HTML file in growth/ (no build step, no external
  assets). It should open from disk and just work.
- Navigation by moving between rooms — links, doors, whatever feels
  right. No minimap that gives the whole plan away; the house should
  be learned by walking.
- Never demolish a room. You may add doors to old rooms to reach new
  ones.
- Each room gets a name and something worth stopping for: a text, an
  object, a view, a sound described.

## Bloom looks like

A dozen-plus rooms that a visitor can get pleasantly lost in for ten
minutes, with an architecture that couldn't have been designed by
anyone who remembered — and a journal that reads like the ship's log
of a building.
